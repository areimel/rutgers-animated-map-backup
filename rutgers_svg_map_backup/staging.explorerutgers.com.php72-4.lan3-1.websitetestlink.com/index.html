Busch

Busch is where ahead-of-the-curve medicine, science, and engineering happens. Students come here to attend classes at our Ernest Mario School of Pharmacy and School of Engineering. And on gamedays in the fall, Busch is electric as everyone hits the campus to cheer on our football team at SHI Stadium. (Go Scarlet Knights!)

SEE CAMPUS

College Ave

This is where it all began – the historic center of the university. From Greek Life, the Honors College, School of Arts and Sciences … to the amazing residential and retail college-community space, The Yard, there’s always something happening on campus. You’ll even find our famous Fat Sandwich here. (Just think of ALL your favorite foods on a sub roll – Yum!) Plus, NYC and Philly are just a short train ride away from the New Brunswick train station (connected to our bookstore).

SEE CAMPUS

Cook

With a focus on all things environmental, this campus is the ideal location for our School of Environmental and Biological Sciences. Here you’ll find greenhouses and a working organic farm with sheep, pigs, cows, and horses. It’s a postcard-perfect setting with lots of open, green spaces.

SEE CAMPUS

Douglass

Douglass shares in the quiet charm of its neighboring campus, Cook. But here, nature’s serenity fuels creative, open expression through the Mason Gross School of the Arts, Nicholas Music Center, and Global Village living-learning communities. Douglass is also home to Douglass Residential College, a close-knit community for women that supports women’s success and leadership at Rutgers and afterwards.

SEE CAMPUS

Downtown-New Brunswick

From four-star restaurants offering a multitude of ethnic cuisines, to comedy clubs, to three theaters, New Brunswick offers the eclectic mix you want in a city. Whether you’re in the mood for Ethiopian fare or catching a band at a club, you’ll be well-fed and entertained. And if you’re considering a career in health care, the city is home to two teaching hospitals, Rutgers Robert Wood Johnson Medical School and the Cancer Institute of New Jersey.

SEE CAMPUS

Livingston

“Livi” is home to a plaza featuring a movie theater and restaurants like Henry’s Diner. There’s even a Starbucks and a Dunkin ... need we say more?! Livingston also houses the Rutgers Athletic Center (The RAC), where you can cheer on the men’s and women’s basketball and wrestling teams. And when it’s time to crunch some numbers, the Rutgers Business School is housed in a stunning new modern building equipped with the latest technology.
